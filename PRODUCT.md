# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mor Handy serves Maryland homeowners, renters, landlords, real estate operators, and busy property managers who need a dependable repair team without calling five separate specialists. Visitors are usually on mobile, often with a visible problem in front of them, and they need fast confidence that the company handles their issue, serves their area, and is easy to book or call.

## Product Purpose

The website turns repair anxiety into booked estimates. It explains Mor Handy's core home repair services, proves real field experience through project imagery, supports local SEO across Maryland service areas, and keeps phone and Calendly booking actions available on every page.

## Brand Personality

Steady, practical, and sharp. The brand should feel like a capable local crew that respects the homeowner's time: professional enough for high-value properties, direct enough for urgent repairs, and warm enough to remove friction from asking for help.

## Anti-references

Avoid childish typography, generic AI landing-page polish, over-rounded template cards, emoji-based visuals, vague stock copy, and pages that feel like a directory instead of a skilled service company. Do not hide conversion actions, over-animate critical content, or use decorative effects that make the business feel less trustworthy.

## Design Principles

Show real work before making big claims.

Make the next action obvious from every viewport.

Use trade-grade confidence: precise copy, durable typography, organized proof, and clear service boundaries.

Design for the mobile homeowner first, then scale up for desktop.

Treat motion as guidance, not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and readable tap targets. Preserve reduced-motion behavior, keep phone and booking links accessible by keyboard and screen reader, use descriptive image alt text, and avoid interactions that block urgent users from calling.
